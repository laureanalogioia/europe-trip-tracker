Warm Scandinavian summer with 18+ hrs of daylight. Mostly sunny with occasional showers that pass quickly. One of the nicest times to visit — long bright evenings.

🇳🇴 Oslo
Jul 5–8
22°C / 12°C (72°F / 54°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 7 rain days · 81mm/mo
Very similar to Stockholm. Long bright days, 22°C highs. July can have short rain bursts but they clear fast. Great weather for the fjord and city.
Pack: Same as Stockholm — layers + light rain jacket.

🇩🇰 Copenhagen
Jul 8–12
22°C / 14°C (72°F / 57°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 9 rain days · 65mm/mo
Warm and sunny but July is actually Copenhagen's rainiest month — expect a shower or two. Nothing heavy, just classic northern European 'it might rain later'. Evenings are long and lovely.
Pack: Light clothes + rain jacket. Layers for evenings.
⛅
🇩🇪 Hamburg
Jul 12–14
23°C / 14°C (73°F / 57°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 11 rain days · 75mm/mo
Warm and pleasant. Can get humid. Hamburg has a reputation for rain year-round but July is one of the better months. Afternoons can be muggy.
Pack: Breathable layers, rain jacket.
🌤️
🇳🇱 Amsterdam
Jul 14–19
23°C / 14°C (73°F / 57°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 11 rain days · 82mm/mo
Peak summer, warm and bright. Can have afternoon thunderstorms that blow over fast. The canals look incredible in summer light. Jul 17 Weeknd concert should be warm.
Pack: Summer clothes, light jacket, small umbrella.
☀️
🇫🇷 Paris
Jul 19–26
25°C / 15°C (77°F / 59°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 11 rain days · 21mm/mo
Late July is one of Paris's hottest stretches. Only 21mm of rain — the driest of all your cities. Heatwaves (35°C+) have become more common. Evenings are warm and golden.
Pack: Light summer clothes, sunscreen, sunglasses. One thin layer for air-conditioned museums.
⛅
🇬🇧 Woodbridge
Jul 26–Sep 1
22°C / 15°C (72°F / 59°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 13 rain days · 60mm/mo
British summer — can be glorious or grey, sometimes both in a day. August is drier than July. Suffolk coast gets a nice sea breeze. Sunny spells are lovely; don't expect Mediterranean heat.
Pack: Light layers, always have a rain jacket. Light hoodie for evenings.
🌦️
🇮🇪 Dublin
Aug 9–14
20°C / 13°C (68°F / 55°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 16 rain days · 81mm/mo
Cool and frequently showery — Ireland is green for a reason. August is one of the drier months relatively, but 16 rain days is still a lot. Showers tend to be short. Sunny Irish days are stunning.
Pack: Waterproof jacket is essential. Layers. No sandals — wet cobblestones.
🌧️
🇮🇪 Galway
Aug 14–17
19°C / 12°C (66°F / 54°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 17 rain days · 108mm/mo
The west coast is windier and wetter than Dublin — 108mm in August vs Dublin's 81mm. Atlantic weather rolls in off the ocean. Expect sun, clouds, showers, then sun again. The Cliffs of Moher in mist are dramatic.
Pack: Waterproof everything. Wind jacket. Waterproof shoes for the cliffs.
🌍 Leg 2 — Southern Europe
🌤️
🇨🇭 Geneva
Sep 1–3
21°C / 11°C (70°F / 52°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 8 rain days · 100mm/mo
Late summer warmth, mostly sunny. Lake Geneva glitters. September brings occasional afternoon storms that pass fast. 100mm total but only 8 rain days — they're heavy when they come.
Pack: Light summer clothes + jacket for evenings.
⛅
🇨🇭 Interlaken
Sep 3–6
18°C / 9°C (64°F / 48°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 17 rain days · 53mm/mo
Alpine — daytime is warm, mornings and evenings are crisp (9°C lows). Mountains catch cloud so conditions shift quickly. High rain days but lower total precipitation than you'd expect. Spectacular setting.
Pack: Layers essential. Warm fleece for evenings. Waterproof jacket.
☀️
🇮🇹 Venice
Sep 6–9
24°C / 14°C (75°F / 57°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 6 rain days · 72mm/mo
Still full summer warmth at 24°C. Only 6 rain days — much drier than August. Crowds thinning fast. One of the best times to visit Venice. Evenings are warm and golden.
Pack: Summer clothes. Comfortable shoes — a lot of walking on uneven stone.
🌤️
🇸🇮 Lake Bled
Sep 9–12
19°C / 10°C (66°F / 50°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 14 rain days · 120mm/mo
Autumn colours starting. Mornings are crisp and misty on the lake — absolutely stunning. Afternoons warm up to 19°C. Getting quieter. 14 rain days but the mist adds atmosphere.
Pack: Layers, warm layer for mornings and evenings.
⛅
🇸🇮 Ljubljana
Sep 12–16
21°C / 12°C (70°F / 54°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 15 rain days · 140mm/mo
September is Ljubljana's second rainiest month (140mm) so pack accordingly. But at 21°C highs it's warm enough for t-shirts during the day. The old town cafe scene is excellent whatever the weather.
Pack: Light layers, rain jacket — Ljubljana gets genuinely wet in September.
⛅
🇭🇷 Plitvice
Sep 16–18
22°C / 11°C (72°F / 52°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 10 rain days · 120mm/mo
Early autumn in the national park — waterfalls are full, early leaf colour, crowds much thinner than summer. 22°C highs but 10 rain days mean pack for it. Crisp and fresh air.
Pack: Layers, rain jacket, comfortable hiking/waterproof shoes.
☀️
🇭🇷 Split
Sep 18–21
25°C / 18°C (77°F / 64°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 6 rain days · 80mm/mo
Full Mediterranean summer at 25°C with only 5–6 rain days. Sea is at its warmest. August crowds are gone. One of the best times on the Adriatic coast.
Pack: Summer clothes, swimwear — sea is perfectly warm.
☀️
🇭🇷 Dubrovnik
Sep 21–26
24°C / 18°C (75°F / 64°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 9 rain days · 139mm/mo
24°C and mostly sunny, though September sees 139mm of rain (it falls hard when it comes). 9 rain days means odds are you'll see one shower. The old town walls in golden autumn light are incredible.
Pack: Summer clothes + light jacket. One rain layer just in case.
🌤️
🇲🇪 Kotor
Sep 26–Oct 1
24°C / 14°C (75°F / 57°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 8 rain days · 104mm/mo
Still warm at 24°C transitioning into October. The bay is beautiful. 8 rain days with 104mm — Kotor is one of the rainiest spots in Europe annually, though September is better than winter.
Pack: Summer clothes + light jacket. A rain layer.
🌤️
🇲🇪 Budva
Oct 1–4
19°C / 13°C (66°F / 55°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 10 rain days · 120mm/mo
Early October on the Adriatic — warm at 19°C, sea still swimmable. Noticeably quieter than summer. Some chance of rain picking up.
Pack: Light layers, jacket for evenings.
🌥️
🇧🇦 Mostar
Oct 4–7
18°C / 8°C (64°F / 46°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 11 rain days · 184mm/mo
Autumn arriving properly. 18°C highs but only 8°C at night. October is Mostar's wettest month at 184mm — the highest of any stop on your trip. The Stari Most bridge in autumn light is still beautiful.
Pack: Warm jacket, layers, waterproof essential. Umbrella.
🌥️
🇧🇦 Sarajevo

16°C / 7°C (61°F / 45°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 10 rain days · 75mm/mo
Getting properly cool — 16°C highs, 7°C at night. Inland and at higher elevation. Can be grey and misty. The city has a melancholic autumn vibe that genuinely suits it.
Pack: Warm jacket, layers, something for rain.
🌤️
🇦🇱 Tirana
Oct 10–12
21°C / 12°C (70°F / 54°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 7 rain days · 105mm/mo
Back to mild and pleasant at 21°C. October in Albania is lovely — warm enough to enjoy outdoors, quiet. More sun than the Balkan interior you just came from.
Pack: Light jacket, layers.
🌤️
🇦🇱 Sarandë
Oct 12–19
22°C / 11°C (72°F / 52°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 9 rain days · 125mm/mo
The Albanian Riviera in October — 22°C, quiet, gorgeous. Sea still swimmable (just). 9 rain days with 125mm but plenty of sunny days between. One of the best-value sun destinations at this time of year.
Pack: Summer clothes + light jacket for evenings.
⛅
🇬🇷 Corfu
Oct 19–23
24°C / 14°C (75°F / 57°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 10 rain days · 180mm/mo
Warmer than you'd expect at 24°C — Corfu stays Mediterranean late into autumn. But October is when the rain starts picking up (180mm). Sunny spells between showers. Very quiet.
Pack: Light layers, rain jacket. Still warm enough for lunch outside.
☀️
🇬🇷 Athens
Oct 23–29
24°C / 16°C (75°F / 61°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 6 rain days · 50mm/mo
One of the best times to visit Athens. 24°C, only 6 rain days, 50mm total. Warm enough for t-shirts, cool enough to walk the Acropolis comfortably without sweating. Low crowds, golden light.
Pack: Light layers, comfortable shoes for the ruins.
⛅
🇬🇷 Mykonos
Oct 29–Nov 1
18°C / 14°C (64°F / 57°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 8 rain days · 70mm/mo
Late October into November — 18°C highs, breezy (Aegean winds pick up). Moody and quiet. Many places start closing for the season. Very different from summer Mykonos.
Pack: Jacket, layers. Check what's open in advance.
⛅
🇬🇷 Paros
Nov 1–5
18°C / 13°C (64°F / 55°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 9 rain days · 80mm/mo
Off-season quiet at 18°C. Can have overcast days and showers but sunny days are still common in early November. Almost entirely to yourself.

🇬🇷 Naxos
Nov 5–9
18°C / 13°C (64°F / 55°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 9 rain days · 80mm/mo
Same as Paros — Naxos has better off-season infrastructure than most Cyclades islands. Village life fully intact, quiet beaches.
Pack: Layers, rain jacket.

19°C / 13°C (66°F / 55°F) hi/lo
🌧 avg 8 rain days · 70mm/mo
